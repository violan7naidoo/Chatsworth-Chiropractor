Back Pain

Back pain is the leading cause of disability worldwide, hence it is vital to know how to prevent back problems. Ways to maintain good back health include:

  • Healthy diet and weight maintenance
  • Remaining active and avoiding prolonged inactivity or bed rest
  • Before doing exercises or any physical activity, warm up and stretch is recommended.
  • Proper posture, wear comfortable, low-heeled shoes and sleep on a mattress of medium firmness to minimize any curvature in your spine.
  • It is always good to remember to lift with your knees, do not twist when lifting and keep objects close to your body.
  • Ensure your working space is ergonomically correct

    • Spinal manipulation and chiropractic care are generally considered safe, effective treatments for acute and chronic back pain.

    Neck Pain

    The neck’s susceptibility to pain and injury is due in part to biomechanics and its flexibility. Activities and events that affect cervical spine biomechanics include extended sitting, repetitive movement, accidents, head injuries and normal aging.
    A neck adjustment (also known as cervical manipulation) is a precise procedure where pressure is applied to the joints of the neck, usually by hand. A neck adjustment works to improve the mobility of the spine and to restore range of motion. Patients typically notice an improved ability to turn and tilt the head as well as a reduction of pain, soreness and stiffness.

    Headaches

    Most headaches are tension, migraine, or cluster headaches.
    Chiropractic care can assist you by performing spinal manipulation or adjustments that improve spinal function to alleviate mechanical stress. Massage therapy will reduce stress and anxiety, relax muscles, rehabilitate injuries, reduce pain and promote overall health and wellness. We also offer advice on your posture, diet, exercises, and relaxation techniques. This can assist you in relieving tension in the muscles of your neck and upper back.

    Sports Injury Assessment and Rehabilitation

    There are a number of ways in which a soft tissue sports injury can occur: Muscle strain – when the muscle or tendon is pulled too far in one direction Contusions – resulting from a direct blow to the muscle/tissue Tendonitis – when the tendon becomes overstressed and inflamed Ligament Sprains – when the ligament is stretched beyond its normal range.
    We have been trained with an extensive knowledge of sports and sports-related injuries. Treatment of sports injuries includes widely used physical (ice/dry needling/strapping) and massage therapies. These techniques can help athletes return to training and perform at their peak level sooner.

    Weight loss programme

    Obesity is not only a chronic disease in itself, but also a major risk factor for the world’s leading causes of poor health and early death including cardiovascular disease, several common cancers, diabetes and osteoarthritis.
    There is no denying that it’s tough to lose weight. There are many confounding variables like current weight, age, sex, metabolism, genetics and medical conditions. It can be difficult to figure out which route will work best for you, especially when there are literally thousands of diets, workout plans, supplements, protein shakes and meal plans out there to choose from.
    Even once you decide on your plan of action, you may find that at a certain point, no matter what you do, you have hit a plateau in your weight loss.
    We specialise in aiding your weight loss with a comprehensive, personalised plan by offering nutritional and exercise advice that works.

    Chronic Disease Management

    Seeking chiropractic care to holistically manage chronic disease is a great option for supporting the body’s natural healing abilities and function.
    Chiropractic care can be helpful for a variety of chronic, or long-lasting, medical conditions. These include non-orthopaedic chronic medical conditions – like diabetes, heart disease, mental illness, lung disease and arthritis.
    We offer health screenings for various conditions including diabetes, hypertension and metabolic syndrome.
    Chiropractic treatments are generally safe, even for patients with chronic illnesses.

    Arthritis

    Arthritis is usually associated with pain, swelling, and reduced range of motion in the affected joint. Arthritis is a general term that affects your joint health, and living with it can be extremely frustrating and debilitating. Symptoms often begin as mild pain that gradually increases in severity over time. Regardless of the type of arthritis (osteoarthritis/rheumatoid arthritis or psoriatic arthritis) you are suffering from, the pain can be overwhelming.
    Chiropractic care has been known to slow down or even stop the progression of arthritis. Not only will you see the benefits in a decrease of pain, but you will also see an improvement in your overall health and movement.

    Other musculoskeletal conditions

    We specialise in treatment of myofascial pain syndrome/fibromyalgia. Symptoms of this syndrome typically involve the muscle or a muscle situated near a joint that is especially painful. Over time, pain can spread to other areas. Trigger points are tightened, hypersensitive spots that can be located in any muscle. One of the best forms of chiropractic therapies for myofascial pain syndrome is myofascial release therapy.
    Trigger point therapy is effective at addressing muscle knots and areas where the muscle has been strained or torn. Through the targeted pressure applied, blood flow is increased to these areas, promoting and accelerating the healing process.
    Other therapies include spinal manipulation and dry needling.
